Preparing Fabric or Yarn


Before you start the dyeing process, it's essential to properly prepare your fabric or yarn. Follow these steps for washing and mordanting:


1. Weighing and Recording


Begin by weighing your dry fabric or yarn and make a note of the amount.


2. Washing Fabric



For fabric, use a washing machine on a 30 or 40Â°C cycle with a natural laundry liquid. Ensure an extra rinse cycle to remove all soap. Alternatively, hand wash, rinse thoroughly, and gently squeeze out excess water.


In the past, scouring fabric with boiling water and soda ash was common, but washing alone can yield excellent results without being too harsh on the material.


3. Washing Yarn


To prevent tangling, wind the yarn into a circle. Untwist a skein or wind a ball around the back of a chair, tying string around the circle in a few places to keep the yarn together.


Wash the yarn in warm water with natural laundry liquid, rinse, and squeeze out excess water.


Ensure not to tie the string too tightly, allowing the yarn to dye evenly under the string.


These steps prepare your fabric or yarn for the dyeing process. It's advisable to wait at least a week before dyeing after completing these preparations. Having a stash of ready-prepared fibers can be convenient for spontaneous dyeing projects.
