Soya Milk Treatment



Before diving into the dyeing process, pretreating your fibers with soya milk can enhance the color absorption. You can either buy
organic unsweetened soya milk or make your own using the following recipe:



Making Soya Milk


  • 125g organic soya beans

  • 1 litre water

  • Soak soya beans in water overnight until swollen.

  • Blend beans and water until smooth.

  • Strain the milk through a muslin cloth, squeezing out excess.

  • Repeat blending and straining a couple more times.


Both store-bought and homemade soya milk work well. Homemade milk, being more concentrated, can mordant more fibers. Look for organic, unsweetened milk with minimal ingredients.



Applying Soya Milk to Fabric and Yarn



  1. Dilute 1 litre of (supermarket) soya milk in a bucket with approximately 5 litres of water. Homemade milk can be further diluted.

  2. Submerge damp fabric in the bucket, ensuring even coating.

  3. Let it soak for about 12 hours, stirring occasionally.

  4. Squeeze out excess milk, spin fabric in the washing machine, and hang to dry.

  5. For yarn, gently squeeze and hang to dry, ensuring a light, even coating.

  6. Store the bucket in a cool place; mix if separated before the next step.

  7. Once dry, briefly dip fabric/yarn into the bucket for an additional coating without rinsing the first layer.

  8. Repeat for a third layer and wait at least a week before dyeing.


This time allows soybean protein to bond to the fibers.


Using a Two-Step Process


While an all-in-one method works, a two-step process yields more even colors:



Preparing Your Plants


Use dyestuff in at least the same weight as fibers. Experiment with quantities for desired color intensity.



Extracting Dye



  1. Place dyestuff in an aluminum pot, cover with water, and heat for about an hour, adjusting heat levels.

  2. Let it cool, strain through muslin, and repeat if desired.

  3. Heat the dye in the pot for an hour to benefit from aluminum mordanting. Leave for at least 24 hours before dyeing.


Experiment with different dyestuffs and extraction methods for diverse colors.
